## Rule 6 — The single most impactful improvement

Avg view duration is 10–20s. The #1 fix: **the opening leads with the product**
instead of the stakes. Move the gut-punch line — *"Can you remember what he did in
the 4th?"* — to **second 0**.

**Exercise:** Write the first line 10 different ways as a direct question to the
parent. Film all 10 as 5-second selfie clips. Keep only the one that makes *you*
pause on rewatch. That's the hook.

## AI video prompts (Sora / Runway / Kling / Veo)

Global: `9:16 vertical, 5 seconds, cinematic, shallow depth of field, natural daylight, realistic`.

- **Shot A (hook):** First-person POV from the stands, a parent's hands holding a phone as if filming, a young boy in uniform steps into the batter's box on a sunlit field, chain-link fence blurred foreground, warm afternoon light, gentle handheld, hopeful.
- **Shot B (scorebook):** Close-up of a worn paper baseball scorebook lying closed on a shelf, thin dust on the cover, soft side window light, faded pencil marks, nostalgic, slow push-in.
- **Shot C (gone):** A hand tosses an old scorebook into a cluttered drawer of forgotten papers, slides it shut, dim indoor light, finality, slight slow motion.
- **Shot D (Angle B payoff):** A father alone in empty metal bleachers at dusk, looking at an empty diamond, golden-hour light, wistful, slow dolly-in from behind, film grain.
- **Shot E (Angle C):** Extreme close-up of a phone showing a paywall "Renew Subscription — $9.99/month," frustrated thumb hovering, cool blue glow, tense.
- **Shot F (reusable):** Slow-motion youth player swinging and making contact, dust in warm backlight, triumphant, lens flare, golden hour, super slow motion.

App demo shots = real screen-recording of the app, not AI.
